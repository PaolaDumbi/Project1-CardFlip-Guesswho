GUESS WHO

Rules of The Game:
The player sees 20 cards with pictures of important African American history figures. The player has to try to recognize who the figure is. On the back of each picture, the player can find the description of the figure.

Madam C. J. Walker was the first Black female millionaire in America. She made her fortune by creating a homemade line of hair care products for Black women. She built an empire selling products directly to Black women, then employing others to sell them.

John Robert Lewis was the chairman of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee (SNCC) from 1963 to 1966. He was an American politician and civil rights activist who served in the United States House of Representatives for Georgia's 5th congressional district from 1987 until his death in 2020.

Malik el-Shabazz
(Malcom X) was an African-American Muslim minister and human rights activist who was a prominent figure during the civil rights movement. A spokesman for the Nation of Islam until 1964, he was a vocal advocate for black empowerment and the promotion of Islam within the black community.

Martin Luther King Jr. was an American Baptist minister and activist who became the most visible spokesman and leader in the civil rights movement from 1955 until his assassination in 1968.

Robert Sengstacke Abbott founded the Chicago Defender weekly newspaper in 1905. Abbott and his newspaper played an integral part in encouraging African Americans to migrate from the South for better economic opportunities.

Oprah Winfrey was the richest African-American of the 20th century, was once the world's only black billionaire, and the greatest black philanthropist in U.S. history. By 2007, she was ranked as the most influential woman in the world.

Barack Hussein Obama is an American politician who served as the 44th president of the United States from 2009 to 2017. A member of the Democratic Party, Obama was the first African-American president of the United States.

Rosa Louise Parks was an American activist in the civil rights movement best known for her pivotal role in the Montgomery bus boycott.

Stevland Hardaway Morris (Steve Wonder) is an American singer, songwriter, musician and record producer. Wonder is credited as a pioneer and influence by musicians across a range of genres that includes rhythm and blues, pop, soul, gospel, funk, and jazz.

Jackie Robinson was an American professional baseball player who became the first African American to play in Major League Baseball. Robinson broke the baseball color line when he started at first base for the Brooklyn Dodgers on April 15, 1947.

Harriet Tubman was an American abolitionist and political activist. Born into slavery, Tubman escaped and rescued approximately 70 enslaved people, using the network of antislavery activists and safe houses known as the Underground Railroad.

Muhammad Ali was an American professional boxer, activist, entertainer, poet and philanthropist.

Maya Angelou is the first Black woman to appear on a quarter. Angelou is prolific writer and poet, and activist is known for her lyrical prose and regal speaking voice.

James Arthur Baldwin was an American writer and activist. As a writer, he garnered acclaim across various mediums, including essays, novels, plays, and poems.

Mae Carol Jemison is a physician, and a former NASA astronaut. She was the first African American woman who orbited into space aboard the shuttle Endeavour in 1992.

Jane Bolin was a pioneer in law, she was the first Black woman to attend Yale Law School in 1931. In 1939, she became the first Black female judge in the United States, where she served for 10 years.

Benjamin O. Davis Sr. was the first Black general in the American military. He served for 50 years as a temporary first lieutenant at an all-Black unit during the Spanish American War
